Introduction

Welcome to Middle School Orchestra class! I'm very excited to be making music with your students this year. Though it may be a little different from what you're used to, this class will focus on developing creative performing musicians, rather than developing those that perform the creative works of others. We'll be exploring lots of different genres, or maybe some new ones that the students will make up! There aren't any requirements for this class other than a willingness to learn and time to practice on their own.

Feel free to contact me any time via email if you have questions. I'll get back to you as soon as I'm back in school, which is between 7AM-4PM

Instrument and Uniform Rentals

Students will be required to bring their own instruments to class. Students can purchase or rent an instrument, whichever is the better option for your situation. I reccommend this instrument rental shop if you decide to rent.

Students will not be required to wear a specific uniform to concerts, but students must wear all black, which is standard for most orchestras.

Auditions

While every student who signs up for the class will be accepted into the orchestra, we will have an informal audition for the role of concertmaster. Any violinist who is interested in auditioning for this role may select a piece from our method book, Essential Elements for Strings and perform it during the second Friday of school. There is no obligation to audition.

Conclusion

  • Positive Learning Environment

    The orchestra classroom will be a positive learning environment for every student. There will be a zero-tolerance policy for bullying and the students will develop their ability to give positive feedback to their classmates. I want my students to enjoy music and enjoy orchestra class, so this will be a very low-stress environment.

  • Rules

    • Arrive to class on time
    • Be prepared for class
    • Be respectful of others
    • Be willing to try
    • Be respectful of the classroom and the instruments
    • Be respectful of your classmates and your teacher

    Failure to follow the rules will result in lost instrument time and a call home

  • Procedures

    • Mute your cellphone and keep it in your pocket unless we're using it in class
    • Retrieve your instrument from the closet before you sit down for class
    • Clean up your area before class ends
    • Place your garbage in the garbage can
    • Do not leave the classroom before I give you permission
    • Be respectful of your classmates and of me

  • My Teaching Philosophy

    When I was a middle school student, I loved my music class. I learned how to improve my technical skill on my violin and I learned how to improve my ability to play in an ensemble. However, the biggest thing my education was lacking was creativity. In my future lessons, I'd like to develop lesson plans that encourage students to use what they know about music to develop their own ideas. This can be a very slow process so many educators find this method to be inefficient because they're focused on preparing a concert to impress parents and administrators, but I believe that it is more important for a student to learn something that will stay with them for a long time.

    However, I am not the first educator to suggest this. Carl Orff was one of the most influential figures in introducing improvisation and creativity to the classroom. In the 1920s, Orff and Dorothee Gunther opened a new kind of music school, one where they focused on learning music through self-discovery and experimenting with music elements. They called this school the Guntherschule. Students that attended the Guntherschule recieved a holistic music education, one that taught them to think independently as musicians and develop their own ideas.

    If a student is able to develop their own ideas in a creative context, they have everything they need to know in order to develop their own ideas in an english class or history class. Being able to read notation and create music from it is not too different from reading a book or news story and developing your own opinion from it.
